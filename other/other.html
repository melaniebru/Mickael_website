Mathematical “poetry”

A couple of “poetical" musings around mathematical themes.
Forgetful pantoum
A pantoum is a formal constraint on the verses of a poem, and for this one (written as an assignment for a Brooklyn Poets class), an additional constraint was to draw the repeating verses from a technical text.
Spline
“Spleen" is a famous poem by Paul Verlaine. "Spline" is a mathematical parody that I wrote a long time ago. Unfortunately, some puns are lost in translation.
The Gram-Schmidt process
This is a silly submission for a very exclusive poetry contest held in 2009 around the theme: "Le procédé d'orthonormalisation de Gram-Schmidt" (the Gram-Schmidt process for orthonormalising).
You can compare with the submission of my former classmate, co-worker, poetry competitor and néanmoins friend Guillaume Dubach.
